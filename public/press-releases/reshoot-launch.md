# ReShoot — Press Release

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

## ReShoot Lets You "Re-Shoot" Any Video Using Your Phone's Motion Sensors

*New iOS app turns landscape footage into polished portrait content — no re-editing required*

**San Francisco, February 2026** — Thirty Seven, Inc. today announced the launch of ReShoot, a free iOS app that lets creators reframe existing videos and photos using their phone's gyroscope. Instead of cropping statically or re-editing in desktop software, users physically move their phone to control a virtual camera over their footage — producing smooth, natural motion that looks like a reshoot.

### The Problem

Every creator has great footage shot in the wrong orientation. Landscape video that needs to be vertical for TikTok, Reels, or Shorts. The options have been static crops (which lose the action), desktop reframing tools (slow and manual), or reshooting entirely. None of these are fast or fun.

### How ReShoot Works

1. **Import** any video or photo from your library
2. **Choose** your output format — 9:16 portrait, 1:1 square, 4:5 Instagram, or custom
3. **Play the video and move your phone** — tilt to pan, rotate to follow the action, pinch to zoom
4. **Export** — ReShoot renders a new video with your motion path baked in, preserving original quality and audio

The result is footage that feels like it was shot by a skilled camera operator, not algorithmically cropped.

### Key Features

- **Gyroscope-powered reframing** with real-time smoothing for buttery-smooth output
- **Touch controls** as an alternative to motion — drag to pan, pinch to zoom
- **Export up to 4K** in HEVC with original audio preserved
- **HDR support** — automatically detects and tone-maps HDR footage
- **Still image animation** — apply motion paths to photos to create dynamic video
- **Motion path export** — save your camera move as FCPXML, After Effects .jsx, JSON, or CSV for professional NLE workflows
- **Voiceover recording** — narrate over footage during capture

### For Professional Editors

ReShoot bridges the gap between quick mobile reframing and professional post-production. Capture a rough motion path on your phone during a commute, then export it as FCPXML or After Effects keyframes to refine on desktop. No other mobile tool offers this workflow.
